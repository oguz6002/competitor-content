## Source Knowledge Base

VideoSurgeon: Ville Vänni (Trauma surgeon) The goal of this approach is to achieve safe surgical exposure and vascular control of the external iliac artery when bleeding cannot be controlled through the femoral approach. This may occur when hemorrhage originates proximally beyond the reach of femoral exposure, when bleeding is profuse, or when anatomy is distorted by infection at groin level. Such scenarios include iatrogenic injury to the proximal femoral arteries, high-energy penetrating trauma, or ruptured pseudoaneurysm from infected groin wounds.
