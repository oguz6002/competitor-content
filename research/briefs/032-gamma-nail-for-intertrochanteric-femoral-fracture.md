Video Surgeon: Lasse Rämö (orthopedic surgeon)  StepsPreparation and fracture reduction The patient is supine on the operating (fracture) table. Either general or spinal anesthesia can be used. The contralateral leg is placed on a leg support and abducted as far as possible to keep it out of the way when taking fluoroscopic images with the C-arm.  The upper body should be bent towards the contralateral side from the fracture to facilitate proper insertion of the nail.
